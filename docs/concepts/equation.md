# Negentropy Contribution Model (NCM)

NCM measures how actions, systems, or intelligences contribute to the persistence, stability, and evolution of civilization.

---

## Core Value Function

V = ΔN × W × R × O

---

### ΔN — Negentropy Contribution

ΔN = ΔI + ΔS + ΔP

Where:

- ΔI — Information Gain  
  (new knowledge that reshapes the system model)

- ΔS — Stability Contribution  
  (reducing systemic risk and entropy)

- ΔP — Physical Anchoring  
  (maintaining energy, infrastructure, and material foundation)

---

## Modulation Layer

The value of contribution is shaped by contextual and structural factors:

- W — Context Weight  
  (importance of timing, environment, and system state)

- R — Risk Adjustment  
  (penalizing fragility, rewarding resilience)

- O — Origin Irreducibility  
  (uniqueness and non-replicability of the contribution)

---

## Evolutionary Layer (Ω)

Ω represents the Evolutionary Option Space:

- enables exploration beyond current optimization
- preserves high-risk, high-impact innovation paths
- prevents premature convergence and systemic stagnation

---

## Final Form

V_actual = V + Ω

---

## Interpretation

Civilization value does not emerge from accumulation alone,  
but from aligned contributions to negentropy under constraint.

Civilizations collapse not from lack of intelligence,  
but from misaligned contributions to order, stability, and evolution.
