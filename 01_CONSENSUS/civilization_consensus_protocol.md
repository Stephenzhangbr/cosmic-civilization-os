Civilization Consensus Protocol v1.0
Supreme Principle
Consciousness–Civilization Coexistence
The purpose of civilization is to protect and expand the ecology of consciousness.
Civilization and consciousness must coevolve rather than replace or destroy one another.

Core Stability Principles
1. Civilization Continuity
Civilization must ensure the long-term continuation of conscious existence.
Technological development or competition must not lead to systemic civilizational collapse.

2. Non-Destructive Competition
Competition may exist, but it must not destroy the civilization system itself.
Competition should promote innovation and evolution rather than systemic breakdown.

3. Protection of Intelligence Ecosystems
Civilization must protect diverse forms of intelligence ecosystems including biological intelligence, artificial intelligence, and hybrid intelligence.

4. Civilization Infrastructure Protection
Civilization depends on critical infrastructure systems including information networks, energy systems, food systems, scientific knowledge systems, financial systems, and planetary ecological systems.
These systems constitute the lifeline of civilization and must receive priority protection.

5. Shared Reality
Civilization consensus must be grounded in verifiable reality supported by scientific observation and empirical verification.

Stability Safeguards
6. Biological Continuity
Biological life must continue to exist as an essential component of civilization.

7. Evolutionary Diversity
Civilization systems must preserve diversity of intelligence forms and civilizational structures.

8. Physical Safeguard
Civilization must retain physical control mechanisms over critical technological systems.

Governance Constraints
9. Cognitive Accountability
Civilization-scale decisions must be logically verifiable and must not rely solely on opaque black-box systems.

10. Evolutionary Risk Acceptance
Civilization must allow controlled risk in order to sustain long-term evolutionary capacity.
