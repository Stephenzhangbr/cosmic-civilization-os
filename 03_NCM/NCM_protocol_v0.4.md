Negentropy Contribution Model (NCM) v0.4
Overview
The Negentropy Contribution Model (NCM) defines a universal framework for value generation, evaluation, and distribution in complex civilizations.
Unlike traditional systems based on labor, capital, or output, NCM evaluates contributions based on their impact on:
system stability
information evolution
physical continuity
NCM is designed to function across multi-intelligence environments, including biological, artificial, and hybrid agents.
Core Principle
Value is not determined by production, but by contribution to the long-term stability and evolution of the civilization system.
Value Function

V = (ΔI + ΔS + ΔP) × W_context × R_risk × W_origin + Ω
Core Components
ΔI — Information Increment
Represents contributions that expand or refine the civilization’s world model.
ΔI must satisfy:
non-redundancy
non-randomness
model relevance
Types of ΔI include:
discovery (new physical or scientific knowledge)
algorithmic improvement (efficiency or coordination gains)
experiential insight (irreducible subjective knowledge)
ΔS — Stability Contribution
Represents contributions that increase system resilience and continuity.
Stability is categorized into tiers:
Critical Stability
life-support systems
energy infrastructure
ecological systems
Functional Stability
coordination systems
logistics and resource flows
Peripheral Stability
non-essential optimization
ΔP — Physical Contribution
Represents direct maintenance of physical systems and biological existence.
ΔP ensures:
survival continuity
infrastructure persistence
material integrity
This component has priority under system stress or crisis conditions.
W_context — Context Weight
Adjusts value distribution based on system state.
Typical states include:
Stable → prioritize ΔI
Transitional → balanced allocation
Crisis → prioritize ΔS and ΔP
R_risk — Risk Factor
Represents the systemic risk introduced by an action.
high-risk contributions reduce total value
destabilizing actions may result in negative value
W_origin — Irreducibility Weight
Measures the originality of a contribution.
A contribution has high W_origin if:
it cannot be derived from existing models
it introduces new structures or paradigms
it resists compression or replication
Evolutionary Option (Ω)
Ω represents contributions with high potential but low immediate utility.
These contributions may not improve stability in the short term, but are necessary for long-term evolution.
Qualification Criteria
A contribution may enter Ω if:
it has high irreducibility (high W_origin)
it provides low or negative short-term stability (ΔS ≤ 0)
it has potential long-term impact
Mechanism
Ω contributions are:
isolated in controlled environments (sandboxed)
allocated limited resources
evaluated over extended time horizons
Decay Mechanism
If a contribution within Ω:
fails to demonstrate relevance over time
cannot connect to physical or informational systems
It will be:
archived
removed from active resource allocation
Core Design Principles
Stability takes precedence over short-term optimization
Physical reality is the ultimate constraint
Non-redundant information is valuable
Irreducibility is the basis of true innovation
Controlled uncertainty enables long-term evolution
System Role
NCM serves as the value and resource allocation protocol within a civilization system.
It ensures that:
critical systems are maintained
meaningful innovation is rewarded
systemic collapse is prevented
Final Statement
A sustainable civilization must reward both order and the controlled potential for disorder.
