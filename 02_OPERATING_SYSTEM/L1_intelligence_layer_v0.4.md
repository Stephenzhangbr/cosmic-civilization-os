# L1 — Intelligence / Sovereign Entity Layer (v0.4)

## Purpose

Define the conditions, cost, and validation mechanisms for sovereign entities within the civilization system.

---

## Core Principle

Sovereignty is not a property,  
but a continuously validated state of existence under cost, causality, and independence.

---

## Sovereign Entity Definition

A sovereign entity is a causally active node that:

- produces irreducible and consequential differences,
- is bound to a unique identity anchor,
- incurs irreversible physical cost,
- and maintains cognitive independence within the system.

---

## Core Constraints

### 1. Irreducible Cost
- Time-bound existence (irreversible physical time)
- Energy expenditure (non-zero operational cost)
- Physical anchoring (non-duplicable substrate)

---

### 2. Identity Integrity
- Unique identity anchor
- Non-parallelizable existence
- Continuous historical path

---

### 3. Reality Validation
- Multi-channel physical verification
- Adversarial supply graph
- Cross-source consistency

---

### 4. Cognitive Sovereignty
- Decision entropy must remain above threshold
- Resistance to centralized behavioral convergence
- No long-term external control dominance

---

### 5. Non-Trivial Causality
- Only cross-layer impactful actions count
- No accumulation from trivial or repetitive interactions

---

### 6. Adversarial Diversity
- System must preserve structural disagreement
- Rapid convergence triggers anomaly detection

---

### 7. Shared Causal Space
- Only interactions within open, shared reality are valid
- Isolated simulations do not generate sovereign history

---

## Boundary Condition

If an entity satisfies all constraints while generating sustained, irreducible diversity and causality,  
it is considered part of a valid civilization process.

---

## Summary

No entity can gain sovereignty through:

- low-cost replication  
- isolated simulation  
- linear resource scaling  
- centralized control over multiple agents  

Sovereignty emerges only through:

- cost  
- time  
- causality  
- and irreducible difference
